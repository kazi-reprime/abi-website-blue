How to Successfully Market Your Barbershop

Open doors don't fill chairs — marketing does. Here is how to use social media, referrals, your website, and discount days to grow a steady barbershop client base.

December 8, 2023

Opening your own barbershop is a major step — it puts you in control of your future. But once your doors are open, the real question becomes:

👉 How do you consistently attract and retain customers?

Marketing is what separates a busy shop from an empty one. Here's how to do it right.

Social Media Is King

Today, social media is one of the most powerful tools for growing your barbershop.

✅ What works:

  • Post consistently (daily or several times per week)
  • Share:
    • Haircuts
    • Transformations
    • Before & afters
    • Shop atmosphere
  • Use short videos (recorded on your phone is perfectly fine)
  • Promote specials, discounts, and events

Always get client permission before posting.

Bottom line: if you're not active online, you're invisible.

Referral & Loyalty Programs

Your existing customers are your best marketing tool.

✅ Referral Program:

  • Offer discounts when clients bring in new customers
  • Turn satisfied clients into promoters

✅ Loyalty Program:

  • Punch cards or digital rewards
  • Example: "Buy 5 haircuts, get 1 free"

Repeat customers are the backbone of your business.

Create a Strong Website

Your website is your 24/7 storefront.

Must include:

  • Services and pricing
  • Business hours
  • Contact information
  • Online booking system
  • Photo gallery (your work)

Pair your website with Google Ads to bring in consistent leads.

Use Discount Days Strategically

Every shop has slower days — use them to your advantage.

✅ Strategy:

  • Offer discounted haircuts on slower weekdays
  • Increase foot traffic during off-peak hours
  • Adjust promotions based on seasons, holidays, and local events

More volume can make up for lower pricing.

Build a Brand, Not Just a Shop

Marketing isn't just about getting customers — it's about building a reputation.

Focus on:

  • Consistency
  • Professionalism
  • Customer experience

When people trust your brand, they come back — and bring others.

Final Thoughts

Marketing your barbershop isn't optional — it's essential.

If you:

  • Stay active online
  • Reward your customers
  • Maintain a professional presence

You will build a steady and growing client base.
