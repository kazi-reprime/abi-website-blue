GI Bill® & VA Education Benefits

ABI accepts VA education benefits. Veterans, service members, and eligible dependents can use their GI Bill® to train at ABI.

Overview
Your Service Earns Your Education
We accept Post-9/11 GI Bill® benefits and other VA education programs to make your training affordable. Our admissions team is experienced with VA benefits and can certify your enrollment quickly.
What VA Benefits May Cover
✂
Tuition and program fees (up to 100% with Post-9/11 GI Bill®)
✂
Monthly housing allowance (Post-9/11 GI Bill®)
✂
Books and supplies stipend up to $1,000/year
✂
Full tuition for eligible VR&E (Ch.31) recipients
✂
Monthly stipend for Montgomery GI Bill® recipients
Programs
VA Education Benefit Programs

Post-9/11 GI Bill® (Ch. 33)

Tuition & fees up to 100%, monthly housing allowance, and a $1,000/year books stipend for those who served after Sept. 10, 2001.

Montgomery GI Bill® (Ch. 30)

Monthly education benefit for veterans who contributed while on active duty.

VR&E (Ch. 31)

For veterans with service-connected disabilities — can cover full tuition plus a subsistence allowance.

DEA (Ch. 35)

Education benefits for eligible dependents of qualifying veterans.

*GI BILL® is a registered trademark of the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs (VA). Eligibility and amounts are determined by the VA, not ABI.
Veterans FAQ
Common Questions
01Is ABI approved to accept GI Bill® benefits?
∧
Yes. ABI is an approved institution for VA education benefits. Contact admissions for our VA facility code.
02How much of my tuition will the GI Bill® cover?
∨
Under Post-9/11 (Ch.33) with 100% eligibility, tuition and fees are covered in full. Other programs pay a monthly stipend.
03How long until benefits start?
∨
After ABI certifies your enrollment, payment typically begins within 4–8 weeks.
04Can dependents use benefits at ABI?
∨
Yes — eligible dependents may use Ch.35 (DEA) or transferred Post-9/11 entitlements.
